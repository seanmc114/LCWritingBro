How The Brother marks your writing

Content — out of 10
Did you actually answer the question? Did you cover the bullet points you were given? If the prompt asks for your opinion and your future plans and you only write about your opinion — you lose marks here.
Accuracy — out of 10
Did you get your Spanish right? Tense agreements, noun-adjective agreement, verb forms, spelling. Every error a Spanish person would notice costs you marks here.
Range — out of 10
Did you use different words and structures, or the same five phrases on repeat? Did you mix your tenses — past, present, future, conditional? This is where H1s separate from H3s. The examiner wants to see variety.
Communication — out of 10
Could a Spanish person read this and understand you? Does it flow naturally? Are you using connectors — sin embargo, además, por eso, aunque? A technically correct answer that reads like a disconnected list loses marks here.
